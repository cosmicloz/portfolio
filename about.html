About Me

As a keen writer and a lifelong science enthusiast, I believe science communication changes the world for the better. My current position with Science Systems and Applications, Inc. assists NASA's Office of the Chief Science Data Officer through the creation and organization of content for NASA’s public science data websites. In improving the experience for visitors to these sites, I hope to support research projects that will teach us more about our planet and our universe.

I graduated summa cum laude from Mount Holyoke College in 2023 with a bachelor's degree in Astronomy and English. During my studies, I spent a summer at Louisiana State University, where I conducted research into the angular diameters of retired A stars for the purposes of furthering exoplanet discoveries around these stars. As I wrote my results into a senior undergraduate thesis, I discovered I enjoy scientific research, but I enjoy communicating about science even more.

My Fall 2023 internship at NASA’s Glenn Research Center further developed my science communication skills. I performed outreach for AI and machine learning efforts at the agency, educating both internal employees and the public about the possibilities for using AI in NASA activities. I am excited to continue supporting the agency in their mission to explore and innovate for the benefit of all.
